George Brown College is a public college located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. It was founded in 1967 and named after George Brown,
a Father of Confederation and the founder of The Globe and Mail newspaper.

The college offers over 160 diploma, certificate, and degree programs in areas such as business, community services, culinary arts,
design, engineering technologies, health sciences, hospitality and tourism, liberal arts and sciences, and preparatory studies.
The college also has partnerships with universities in Canada and abroad, allowing students to earn a degree after completing 
a diploma or certificate program.

George Brown College has three main campuses located in downtown Toronto: St. James Campus, Casa Loma Campus, and Waterfront Campus.
The college also operates several satellite locations across the city.

The college has a diverse student population, with students from over 100 countries. It is known for its hands-on, experiential
learning approach and its commitment to providing students with the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in their chosen careers.
